Meaning & Origin

"Rudra" is a Sanskrit name traditionally associated with a Vedic deity of storms, the hunt, and wild, roaring power. Over time, Rudra became closely linked with the god Shiva — representing both destruction and renewal.

Core meaning

"The Roarer" or "Howler" — a forceful, elemental presence.

Origin

Sanskrit / Vedic (Indian subcontinent)

Personality traits

Fierce Protective Transformative Independent

Stories & symbolism

In early Vedic texts Rudra is described as a wild and unpredictable deity — later mythologies soften and weave him into the role of Shiva, the cosmic destroyer who clears the way for rebirth.
